EE - 538 Principles of Modern Digital Communication

Communication in simple words can be defined as an act of exchange of information. From using pigeons for delivering messages, to the advent of 5G/ 6G networks, there had been several advancements made for this evolution to take place. From time to time, engineers, and researchers from all around the world have collaborated in defining cellular standards and realized the dream of making this world a single connected place!

Currently, we are witnessing the 5th Generation technology standard for cellular networks. I still remember the 2.5th Generation back in my home country India, when I connected to the Internet for the first time through GPRS/ EDGE technology. I was thrilled to explore the world of the internet with 100-150 Kbps of speed and it felt magical. Fast forward to today, we can browse the internet with a peak speed of 20 Gbps. As each decade passed, we were able to witness a new generation of cellular standards and this was possible due to the research and development in Digital Communication.

The Modern Digital Communication course introduced us to the basic principles that are necessary to design a digital communication system. Instructor Dr. Su taught us various modulation schemes, transmitter design, channel models as well as receiver design. The Professor would actively ask questions throughout the lectures and ignited a spark of curiosity in us. I liked these discussions because they enhanced my critical thinking and intrigued me. The Professor always welcomed to ask any doubts regarding the subject and appreciated it. Assignments were given periodically, which helped us to get a better grip on the subject.

We simulated a digital communication system in MATLAB, to analyze the performance of 3 modulation schemes as the final project for this course. The three modulation schemes used were Binary Pulse Amplitude Modulation (B-PAM), 4-PAM, and 4-Quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM). We compared the simulation results with the theoretical performance of the modulation schemes and collated the performance of three modulation schemes with each other.
