EE 598 - Individual Problem

Individual Problem is an intriguing course option offered by the UB curriculum where students can take part in research projects under the guidance of professors. What a great concept! As soon as I learned about this opportunity, I sent Dr. Filippo Malandra, one of my professors whom I admire and wanted to work with, a cold email to see if there were any projects I might participate in to gain some research experience. After a week, Dr. Malandra finally offered to discuss my interests and guided me to choose one of several fascinating ongoing projects to work on. That is how my work with Dr. Malandra began in the Fall 2021.

I was delighted to be a part of a research project. We developed an LTE-enabled Micro Phasor Measurement Unit(μPMU) and tried to analyze the performance of the Narrow Band IoT network. A phasor Measurement Unit (PMU) is a device that estimates the magnitude and phase of an electrical quantity in an electricity grid using a common time source for synchronization. PMU enables real-time monitoring of an electrical grid and helps in detecting disturbances in the power grid. A Phasor Data Concentrator (PDC) was deployed which aggregates the phasor data streamed from multiple PMUs for enhanced post-event analysis.

In our project, we developed a low-cost, low-complex μPMU using an ARM cortex microcontroller. Network Time Protocol was used for the time synchronization across the system. Several μPMU's were deployed and each one relayed its phasor data along with a timestamp to the PDC through User Datagram Protocol. PDC was placed on a Linux system using Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud service. Multiple tests were conducted simultaneously for the delay analysis. Based on the difference between the phasor's timestamp and the arrival time at PDC, we examined the delay involved in the network. We were able to achieve a delay of less than 200ms. Individual problem introduced me to the realm of research at the University at Buffalo. We worked in a team and had constant feedback from the Professor. We learned to work as a cohesive unit and to collaborate in accomplishing the tasks. There were some disagreements among the team members that were resolved by discussing viewpoints and taking action to find common ground. I am glad that I took this course and would suggest everyone make use of this wonderful prospect that the University is offering.
