EE 620 - MIMO Wireless Communication

MIMO is an incredible technology. Ever wonder why the number of antennas in the home router got increased? How do multiple antennas increase the data rates? The answer to all of these questions lies in MIMO. MIMO stands for Multiple Input Multiple Output. MIMO is one of the reasons how we achieve a blazingly fast internet connection and stream 1080p HDTV with bare minimum latency. MIMO was initially introduced for Wi-Fi systems and now it is used in both Wi-Fi and Cellular standards. Through MIMO, multiple antennas are deployed at both transmitter and receiver which enhances the throughput due to the spatial diversity.

MIMO is one of the challenging courses that I took in my graduate coursework. As I learned the fundamentals of digital communication from EE 538, I was eager to enroll in this course which was a continuation of the former. A brief introduction to various cellular generations and wifi standards was discussed in the initial classes. Later, the RF signal propagation model with a focus on Path Loss, Multipath Fading, and Shadowing was introduced. We also got to know about various channel fading models and popular ST modulation techniques which helped us to gain a sense of why MIMO is important and how it can be implemented. At the end of the coursework, we realized maximum achievable diversity of MIMO-OFDM systems and comprehended the applications of MIMO techniques. The course was detail-oriented with mathematical equations and it helped us to attain practicality along with an intuitive understanding of the subject.

Assignments for the coursework were MATLAB simulations. These simulations introspected our understanding of the concepts that were discussed in the class. Several hours were spent on completing the assignments by constantly revisiting lecture notes and this only further strengthened my understanding of the subject. For some assignments, I collaborated with fellow students to brainstorm the concepts involved and we collectively tried to improve our understanding of the topic.
